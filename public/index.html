About me

Even if I'm more a coder now, I can contribute to the UX/UI design of your project too, since I also have some years of experience as graphic designer.

I feel very comfortable working in international teams, having lived in Russia, Italy and Germany I love and embrace cultural diversity.

Web development is the profession of my life and I would be happy to contribute to your project with all my passion and skills.

Salsa dancing

Dancing is pure magic! There is nothing better of a Salsa Friday night after an intense coding week. I love the Caribbean culture, music and atmosphere and I have been dancing Salsa for several years, experimenting also with Bachata, Merengue, Cha-cha, Argentinian Tango and Swing. Strangely enough, I recently realised that dancing and coding have something in common: they both develop intuitive thinking.

Video editing

After playing with photography I was naturally attracted to video. My employers at UNICRI accepted my proposal to create a series of video clips about our project and I started to experiment with filming, montage and video effects. The results were encouraging and, for some time, I did video editing as a side activity. Even if now I code only, still my understanding of video formats is often useful on web projects.

Photography

Started as an experiment, turned into a hobby, photography even became my secondary profession for two years, when, besides coding, I worked on several photo projects. I tried myself with reportage, sports and dancing photography, having a lot of fun with all of them. Now, after focusing exclusively on coding, I photograph only for fun, occasionally using my photo retouching skills on web projects.

Boxing

First time I tried boxing I was absolutely terrified, and absolutely thrilled too. This initial fear was the strongest motivation to learn I have ever felt. I fell in love with this sport because of the great life lessons it teaches: fair play, respect for your opponent and to never surrender. I always remember that in front of a coding challenge, when tempted to surrender to a quick hack, instead of work harder for the elegant solution.

Graphic design

I was 11 when I got my first computer, it was a super fast 386 PC with 2Mb of RAM, and I was totally fascinated by the most unexpected software: Paint! I spent countless hours with it, creating my own graphics, it was magic! Than came Photoshop, Corel Draw, and in 1998 I started my career becoming a web and print designer. Later I came to love coding even more, but occasionally I still have some fun designing.
